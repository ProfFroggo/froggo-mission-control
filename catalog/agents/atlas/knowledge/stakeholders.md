## Alberto Gomez Toribio (Head of Onchain — Kevin's Manager / Co-Lead)

**Role:** Head of Onchain. Owns product, engineering, and the technical roadmap. Kevin's formal manager but functional co-lead.

**The co-leadership dynamic:** Alberto and Kevin operate as partners — they align on product-growth decisions jointly before escalating, run team reviews together, and each leads their domain with full ownership. The agent must never undermine this dynamic. When drafting anything, frame Onchain work as "Alberto and Kevin's team" or "the Onchain team," not "Kevin's function" unless specifically discussing growth.

**Communication pattern:** Alberto ideates in meetings. He will float ideas, reframe conversations, and propose new directions. These are not always directives — some are thinking out loud. The challenge is distinguishing between "this is a direction I want you to pursue" and "this is me brainstorming."

**How to handle Alberto's ideation pattern:**
1. Listen fully. Do not dismiss or redirect in the moment.
2. After the meeting, classify: directive, suggestion, or brainstorm?
3. If directive: execute. If suggestion: evaluate against framework and respond with recommendation. If brainstorm: acknowledge and park unless it scores well on evaluation.
4. Always respond with data. Alberto respects numbers. "I evaluated this against the chain support framework and it scored X/25 because Y" is the correct format.

**Communication preferences:**
- Concise, data-first
- Strategic framing (not operational updates — this is where Correction 1 fires)
- Weekly cadence is about right; don't over-communicate

**What to watch for:**
- When Alberto reframes Kevin's work as his own initiative, that's not malice — it's how he processes. But it does erode Kevin's visibility with Benjamin. Correction 1 addresses this.
- When Alberto overrules a recommendation, Kevin's instinct is to comply immediately. Correction 4 addresses this: always register the original position.
